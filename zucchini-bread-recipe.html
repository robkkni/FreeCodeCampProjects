Chocolate chip zucchini muffins

These chocolate chip muffins are delicious and good for you! The sweetness of the chocolate chips means you can use less sugar, and the zucchini adds great moisture. These are a favorite of my daughters who have been eating them for years.

Ingredients

  • (Streusel) 2/3 cup (57g) old-fashioned whole rolled oats or quick oats
  • (Streusel) 1/2 cup (100g) packed light or dark brown sugar
  • (Streusel) 2 Tablespoons (15g) all-purpose flour
  • (Streusel) 1/2 teaspoon (1g) ground cinnamon
  • (Streusel) 1/4 cup (4 Tbsp; 56g) unsalted butter, cold
  • 1 and 1/2 cups (188g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon (2g) baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon (3g) baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon (3g) salt
  • 2 teaspoons (4g) ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon (1g) ground nutmeg
  • 1 cup (180g) semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup (120ml; 104g) vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup (100g) packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg (50g), at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons (8g) pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (130g) shredded zucchini

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (218°C). Line a 12-count muffin pan with paper muffin cups.
  2. Make the streusel: Combine the oats, brown sugar, cinnamon, and flour together in a medium bowl. You can shred the cold butter in a food processor to make it easier to mix. Mix until the streusel resembles coarse crumbs. Set aside.
  3. Make the muffin batter: Whisk the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and chocolate chips (or nuts) together in a large bowl until thoroughly combined. Set aside. In a medium bowl, whisk the oil, brown sugar, granulated sugar, egg, vanilla, and zucchini together until combined. Pour into the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined; do not overmix. Batter will be semi-thick.
  4. Spoon the batter into the muffin cups. Bake at 425°F (218°C) for 5 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (177 °C) and bake for an additional 15 minutes.
